## Honest Assessment

5 weeks is not enough to properly prepare for a full marathon from zero base. A standard plan is 16–20 weeks. You're doing this in 5. That said, you have two things going for you:

1. **Aerobic memory** — your cardiovascular system retains some adaptation from your half marathon
2. **Mental toughness** — you ran a sub-2h half with almost no training, which shows grit

This plan is built around one principle: **get to the start line healthy and finish the race on your feet.** This is a survival plan, not a performance plan. Time goal is secondary — finishing is the goal.

### Goal Finish Time
**Sub-4 hours** (pace: ~5:35–5:41/km). This is your half marathon pace for double the distance — aggressive but in the realm of possibility given your fitness history. Decision point: Week 4 long run. If HR and pace hold, we go for it. If not, we adjust to 4:15.

### Risk Acknowledgment
- Higher than normal risk of: shin splints, IT band syndrome, Achilles tendinitis, blisters, and general overuse injuries
- You MUST listen to your body. Sharp pain = stop. No exceptions.
- The run/walk strategy on race day is not a weakness — it's what will get you to the finish

### Philosophy
- **No speed work.** There's no time to build speed safely. Every run is easy or long.
- **Run/walk method** for long runs — this protects your undertrained joints and muscles.
- **Build long run distance aggressively but carefully** — you need your legs to know what 30+ km feels like.
- **Two rest days per week minimum.**
- **Cross-training counts** — cycling or swimming on non-run days to build aerobic fitness without impact.

## Race Day Strategy: The Run/Walk Method

This is not optional for your situation — it's the strategy.

### Strategy: Negative Split — Walk Only at Aid Stations
No scheduled run/walk intervals. Run steady at race pace. Walk only at aid stations (~30–45 sec each, ~8 stations = ~5 min total walking time). Your running pace of 5:35/km compensates for the station walks.

### Pacing Plan (Sub-4h Target)

| Segment | Target Pace | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Km 0–3 | 5:45–5:50/km | DOWNHILL. Don't bank time — bank energy. Hold back. |
| Km 3–10 | 5:35–5:40/km | Settle into race pace. First gel at km 5. Find a pace group. |
| Km 10–21 | 5:35–5:40/km | Cruise. Gels on schedule. This should feel controlled, not hard. |
| Km 21 (halfway) | Check-in | Target split: ~1:58–1:59. If on pace and feeling good, keep going. |
| Km 21–30 | 5:35–5:45/km | The real race begins. Stay disciplined. Keep eating. |
| Km 30–35 | 5:45–6:00/km | **The Wall.** Glycogen depleting. Gels critical. 1km at a time. |
| Km 35–39 | 5:45–6:10/km | You have ~10 min buffer if on pace. Don't panic at slight slowdown. |
| Km 39–42 | 5:50–6:15/km | UPHILL. 50m climb. Dig deep. Crowd carries you. Empty the tank. |
| Finish | < 4:00:00 | Hotel Moskva. Sub-4. |

**Halfway split target: ~1:58–1:59.** If you're at 2:02+ at halfway, sub-4 is still possible but you'll need a perfect second half.

### Aid Station Strategy
- Belgrade Marathon has aid stations approximately every 5km
- WALK through every aid station. Drink properly. Don't spill water on yourself trying to run and drink.
- Take water AND sports drink (alternate)
- Carry your own gels — don't rely on what the course provides

## Recovery & Injury Prevention

### This Matters More Than Usual
With 5 weeks and aggressive volume buildup, recovery is the difference between making it to the start line healthy and getting injured in week 3.

### After EVERY Run
1. Walk 5 min (don't just stop)
2. Drink 500ml water/electrolyte immediately
3. Foam roll — calves, quads, IT band, glutes (10 min)
4. Stretch — hamstrings, hip flexors, calves, quads (10 min)
5. Eat within 30–45 min (carbs + protein)
6. Cold water on legs (shower) if inflamed

### Strength Circuit (2x/week — Tue and Thu/Fri)
Keep it simple and focused on injury prevention:

**2 rounds (not 3 — you need leg freshness for running):**
1. Single-leg glute bridges — 12 each side
2. Calf raises (slow: 3 sec up, 3 sec down) — 15 each side
3. Side-lying leg raises (hip abductors) — 15 each side
4. Bodyweight squats — 15
5. Plank — 30–45 sec
6. Dead bug — 8 each side

**Stop strength training after Week 3.** Weeks 4–5 are for running and resting only.

### Sleep
**8+ hours per night. Non-negotiable.** This is when your body repairs. In a compressed plan, sleep is your most powerful recovery tool.

### Warning Signs — STOP Running If:
- Sharp pain that alters your gait
- Pain that worsens as you run
- Swelling anywhere
- Pain that lasts >24 hours after a run

**Better to arrive at the start line slightly undertrained than injured and unable to start.**

## Race Day: The Complete Guide

### The Week Before (April 12–18)
- **Taper:** Already built into the plan. Volume drops to ~20km.
- **Carb loading (April 16–18):** Increase carb intake to 8–10g per kg bodyweight. This means more pasta, rice, bread, potatoes. Not "eat everything" — specifically increase carbs.
- **Hydrate:** Extra water + electrolytes all week.
- **No alcohol** from Wednesday onward.
- **No new food** — eat only things your stomach knows.
- **Wednesday Apr 16:** Pick up race bib at the Expo (likely at Sava Centar). Walk around, soak in the atmosphere, buy any last-minute gear.
- **Friday Apr 18:** Lay out ALL gear. Pin bib on singlet. Charge watch. Set TWO alarms. Sleep by 9–10 PM.

### Race Morning Checklist (Sunday, April 19)
- [ ] Wake up at 5:00 AM (3h before 8 AM start)
- [ ] Breakfast: toast + jam/honey, banana, coffee (if you normally drink it), 500ml water
- [ ] Body Glide/Vaseline: nipples, inner thighs, underarms, feet, waistband area
- [ ] Tape nipples (medical tape or NipGuards)

- **Km 30–35 will be the hardest thing you've ever done.** This is expected. Keep moving.
- **Km 39–42 is uphill.** You know this. It's only 50m of elevation. Walk if needed. The crowd will be there.

## The Unknown Unknowns

### 1. Bathroom Logistics
- "Runner's stomach" (diarrhea, nausea) is extremely common in marathons
- Avoid high-fiber foods 48h before the race
- Know that there are toilets on the course — if you need to go, GO. Losing 2 min > 40km of misery.
- Caffeine gels can accelerate this. If you use caffeinated gels, test them in training first.

### 2. Toenails
- You may lose toenails from the long runs. Normal for distance runners.
- Trim them SHORT on Wednesday before race day (not Friday — too sensitive).
- Your shoes should have a thumb's width of space at the toe.

### 3. Chafing Will Happen in Places You Don't Expect
- Nipple bleeding (men): tape them. Every run over 15km.
- Inner thighs, underarms, waistband, anywhere skin meets skin or fabric.
- Body Glide liberally before every long run and on race day.

### 4. The Mental Game Is 50% of the Marathon
- Km 0–21: "This is easy, why was I worried?"
- Km 21–30: "This is getting hard but I can manage."
- Km 30–35: "Why did I do this. I should stop. Everything hurts." ← THIS IS NORMAL. Everyone feels this.
- Km 35–42: "I'm going to finish this no matter what."
- **Have a mantra.** Something short. "Keep moving." "One more km." "I chose this."
- **Dedicate kilometers to people.** Km 30 is for your mom. Km 35 is for your best friend. It helps.

### 5. You Will Want to Quit Between Km 30–35
- Your glycogen stores deplete around km 30. This is "the wall."
- Your brain will send every signal telling you to stop.
- This is a nutrition problem as much as a fitness problem — KEEP EATING GELS.
- Break it down: you're not running 12 more km. You're running to the next km marker. Then the next one.

### 6. Pacing Discipline in the First 10km
- The #1 mistake in every marathon: going out too fast because you feel amazing.
- You will feel amazing at km 5. Everyone does.
- If you run km 0–10 at 6:00/km instead of 6:45/km, you WILL pay for it at km 32. Guaranteed.
- Let people pass you. They will walk past you at km 35.

### 7. Belgrade-Specific Course Tips
- The start is DOWNHILL (60m over 3km). Don't let gravity speed you up.
- New Belgrade is flat but exposed — no shade. If it's sunny, wear a cap and sunscreen.
- Bridge crossings are windy. Use other runners as windbreaks.
- The finish is UPHILL (50m over 2km). Save something for this — you'll need it.
- The crowd support in the old city (start/finish) is great. New Belgrade is quieter — bring your own motivation (music, mantra).

### 8. Weather on Race Day (April 19)
- Typical Belgrade mid-April: 8–18°C, possible rain
- Best case: 10–14°C, overcast — perfect marathon weather
- Worst case: 22°C+ and sunny — add 15–30 sec/km to your pace, drink more
- Dress for the temperature at km 20, not km 0. You'll warm up.
- Check the forecast daily from April 16 onward.

### 9. What to Wear If It Rains
- Singlet + shorts (same as dry weather — you'll get wet regardless)
- Optional: very light rain jacket for the first 5km, then discard
- **Vaseline on feet** to reduce moisture friction
- **Plastic bag over shoes** at the start line to keep feet dry until gun time

### 10. Post-Race: What Happens to Your Body
- You will shuffle-walk for 2–3 days. Stairs will be your enemy.
- Take at least 7–10 days completely off running.
- Eat well, sleep well, hydrate. Your immune system is suppressed for 3–5 days after a marathon.
- Post-race blues are real — you've had this massive goal and now it's done. Plan something to look forward to.

### 11. Race Day Logistics
- **Get to Republic Square by 7:00 AM.** The area gets crowded.
- **Bag drop:** The marathon usually has a bag drop service. Use it — bring warm clothes for after.
- **After the finish:** You'll get a medal, a mylar blanket, and food/drink. WALK. Don't sit down immediately — your legs will seize up.
- **Meeting point:** Designate a spot with family/friends BEFORE the race. Cell service will be unreliable.
- **Getting home:** You will NOT want to walk far. Arrange a ride or know the closest taxi/transit stop to the finish.

### 12. Things to Do in the Days Before
- **Study the course map** (available on bgdmarathon.org). Know where the aid stations are, where the bridges are, where the uphills are.
- **Plan your gel schedule** against the course km markers.
- **Drive or walk parts of the course** if possible — familiarity reduces race-day anxiety.

### 13. Don't Change Anything on Race Day
- NOTHING new. Not shoes, not socks, not shorts, not gels, not breakfast.
- The shirt you race in should have at least 3 training runs on it.
- The gels you carry should be the same ones you used on your 28–30km long run.

### 14. You Might Need to Run/Walk More Than Planned
- If at km 25 your run/walk ratio of 10:2 isn't working, switch to 5:2 or even 3:2.
- **Finishing is the only goal.** There is no shame in walking. At km 35, many people around you will be walking.
- A 5:30 finish where you walked half of it still gets you the same medal and the same 42.195km.

### 15. Laundry and Logistics
- You'll be running 4–5 days/week. That's a lot of sweaty kit.
- Rinse gear immediately or it will stink permanently.
- Have at least 2 sets of running clothes so you're never waiting on laundry.

## Training Routes from Banovo Brdo

You live in one of the best spots in Belgrade for running. Košutnjak forest is ~1.5km away, Ada Ciganlija is ~1.5–2km away, Topčider Park is ~3km away. All routes are linear or point-to-point — no repetitive laps. Max 1 lap on Ada Ciganlija (the 8km lake loop).

### Route 1: Košutnjak Forest (5–7km) — Easy/Recovery
- **Out the door → Košutnjak (~1.5km) → forest trails (2–4km) → home (~1.5km)**
- **Trim staza (tartan track):** 1.2km loop. Soft surface. Great for easy days.
- **Forest trails:** Hilly, soft ground, shaded. Perfect for protecting your joints during base building.
- **Best for:** Easy runs, recovery runs, Adios Pro 4 break-in runs.
- **Total distance:** 5–7km depending on how deep into the forest you go.

### Route 2: Ada Ciganlija Single Loop (11km) — Medium
- **Banovo Brdo → Ada (~1.5km) → 1 lake loop (8km) → home (~1.5km)**
- Flat, paved path around the lake. Water fountains along the way.
- **Total distance:** ~11km. No repeated laps.
- **Best for:** Medium-distance runs, easy long efforts.

### Route 3: Topčider–Sava Out-and-Back (10–12km) — Medium
- **Banovo Brdo → Topčider Park (~3km) → continue to Sava river (~2km) → along Sava embankment (0.5–1km) → turn back the same way**
- Mix of park trails and flat river embankment. Scenic, varied terrain.
- **Total distance:** 10–12km depending on how far along the embankment you go.
- **Best for:** Mid-distance runs with some variety and gentle hills.

### Route 4: Ada + Sava Extension (14km) — Long
- **Banovo Brdo → Ada (~1.5km) → 1 lake loop (8km) → continue along Sava embankment (~1.5km) → turn back → home (~1.5km)**
- After completing the Ada loop, instead of heading home, continue along the Sava embankment south/west for extra distance before turning back.
- **Total distance:** ~14km. Linear route with one Ada lap and a river extension.
- **Best for:** Week 2 long run.

### Route 5: Ada + Sava Embankment (21km) — Half Marathon Distance
- **Banovo Brdo → Ada (~1.5km) → 1 lake loop (8km) → Sava embankment south (~3km) → turn back along embankment (~3km) → cross back through Topčider area → home (~3km)**
- OR: **Banovo Brdo → Topčider Park (~3km) → Sava river (~2km) → Sava embankment all the way (~5km one way) → turn back (~5km) → Topčider → shortcut home via Košutnjak (~3km)**
- **Total distance:** ~21km. Long, mostly flat, varied scenery.
- **Best for:** Week 3 long run (half marathon rehearsal). Practice gels on this route.

### Route 6: The Big One — 28–30km Race Rehearsal (Week 4)
- **Banovo Brdo → Ada (~1.5km) → 1 lake loop (8km) → cross Gazela or Ada bridge to New Belgrade → run along New Belgrade Sava embankment toward Zemun (~8–10km) → turn back when you hit ~15km mark → return across bridge → home (~1.5km)**
- This simulates the flat marathon course through New Belgrade. The New Belgrade Sava embankment is ~10km one way toward Zemun — flat, exposed, long straights, just like race day.
- **Total distance:** ~28–30km. Adjust the turnaround point on the New Belgrade side to hit your target distance.
- **Best for:** Week 4 peak long run. Full race-day rehearsal — practice gels, hydration, run/walk ratio, pacing on flat exposed terrain.
