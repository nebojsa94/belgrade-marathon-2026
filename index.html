⚑ Course Profile

Km 0–3 ↓ 60m downhill Bulevar kralja Aleksandra to the river. DON'T go fast — gravity will tempt you.
Km 3–39 ↔ FLAT Two laps through New Belgrade. Flat, long straights, bridge crossings (windy!).
Km 39–42 ↑ 50m uphill Climb back to old city. Finish at Hotel Moskva is uphill.

Bridges

Expect wind. Tuck behind other runners to draft.

New Belgrade

Flat but exposed — no shade. Cap + sunscreen if sunny. Thinner crowd support.

The Finish

50m climb over 2km. Crowd support is electric. Save something for this.

Apple Watch — Is It Enough?

Yes. GPS is accurate for training. Wrist HR works well for zone running. Use the Workout app with Outdoor Run. Enable heart rate zones in the workout view. Pair with Strava for logging. Save €250+ on a Garmin.

  • Tip: Calibrate it — do a 20min run on a flat, open area with good GPS signal
  • Tip: Wear it snug (1 finger width above wrist bone) for best HR
  • Race day: Enable Power Save mode to ensure battery lasts 5+ hours

? The Unknown Unknowns

The Wall (Km 30–35)

Glycogen depletes. Brain screams STOP. Normal. Keep eating gels. 1km chunks. Have a mantra.

Bathroom Issues

Runner's stomach is common. No fiber 48h before. If you need to go — GO.

Chafing & Nipple Bleeding

Body Glide everywhere. Tape nipples. Test on long runs.

Pacing Discipline

#1 mistake: too fast in km 0–10. 6:00 instead of 6:45 early = death at km 32.

Toenails

May lose them. Trim SHORT. Cut Wednesday before race.

Nothing New on Race Day

Not shoes, socks, shorts, gels, or breakfast. Everything tested.

Mental Game (50%)

Have a "why." Dedicate km to people you love. "One more km" is all that matters at km 33.

Weather

April 19: typically 8–18°C. Dress for km 20, not km 0. Check forecast from Apr 16.

Post-Race

Shuffle-walk 2–3 days. 7–10 days off. Immune system suppressed. Rest.

Walking Is OK

5:30 with lots of walking = same medal, same 42.195km.

No Alcohol

Destroys recovery. Zero from Wednesday before race. Limit all 5 weeks.

Sleep > Everything

Night-before sleep will be bad — OK. Thursday night matters more.
